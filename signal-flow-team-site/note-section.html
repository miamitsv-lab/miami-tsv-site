TermMeaning / Note
Run-of-showThe full order of the video from opening to closing.
StoryboardA scene-by-scene guide showing visuals, graphics, avatar scenes, transitions, and timing.
Animation scene listA checklist of every animated scene, diagram, label, avatar movement, and transition needed.
Avatar movementSimple host/guest movement such as mouth movement, blinking, and small head nods.
Signal flow pathThe audio path from microphone/input through mixer/processing and out to speakers/WebEx/streaming.
Diagram labelsShort text used to identify microphones, mixers, cables, outputs, speakers, and WebEx feed.
CalloutsArrows, boxes, or short notes pointing to important signal flow details.
Visual proof testA short test to confirm the diagram style, avatar movement, labels, and readability before full production.
Simple production ruleKeep diagrams readable and use simple movement. Clarity is more important than complex animation.
Current project statusPre-production is closed/locked. Animation work has started, title card and lower-thirds work has started, and the animation scenes are finalized.
Pre-production lockAfter the lock, do not add a new format, new visual style, major script rewrite, or added scenes unless approved as necessary.
Allowed after lockAccuracy fixes, readability fixes, missing assets, light timing improvements, and minor clarity improvements.
Animation production passThe active build stage where finalized scenes are animated, exported, reviewed, and handed off for editing.
Final animation scene listThe approved scene checklist. Use it as the build map so the team stays on schedule and avoids late creative changes.
Title cardThe opening visual card for the Signal Flow video. It should match the TSV color schema, logo treatment, and approved visual style.
Lower thirdsName/role or section-label graphics placed near the lower part of the screen. They should be readable and consistent across scenes.
Missing-asset listA single list of remaining graphics, labels, exports, pickups, or review items needed before the rough cut.
